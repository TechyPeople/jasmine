Connections matter.

We all know the dreaded feeling of doubt-- that we can't handle all this work anymore and that we're bound to crumble under all this stress. It’s when we hit these low points in life that connections matter the most.

That's why I'm proposing a different approach to junior year: one where we, as a bonded class, can support each other and thrive in our toughest year of high school yet.

A Basic Foundation.

A strong class deserves the best basic fundamentals to keep our class running smoothly.

These shouldn't be things you have to fight for: they're your rights.

Communication

Now equipped with a super great website, I believe we can help unlock it's full potential by sending all student frequent reminders to keep them in the loop of current events. More extensive use of the website will help those students lacking access to our Facebook group a chance to keep up.

Transparency

Our current class officers are doing an amazing job with class transparency, and I'll carry on their legacy by starting the year off with more frequent updates on what's going behind the scenes.

Fundraising

Money is going to be an essential part of our junior year to fund events like Homecoming with the additional of Junior Prom. But I want to make fundraisers more than just money-- I want to provide our class with opportunities to connect and make those priceless relationships that will sure to help each of you greatly down the road.

What's New?

I've got some innovative new ideas as well, which are going to take our class to the next level.

Banquets

Held in the evening, banquets will consist of a great dinner to fill you up while your fellow classmate perform for you. It's a great way to bond and hang out with friends and creates opportunities for those wanting to step out of their comfort zone and strut their stuff! Again, another great way to fundraise(it’s not that we care about money--we ultimately want to provide you with the greatest junior prom experience next year!!).

Class Merchandise

Why only have class apparel when you can get anything from posters and wallets to lanyards and stickers?!! Not only can we sport class spirit in style, but this creates numerous opportunities for fundraising.

Service

Might sounds like something right out of a club, but what I have in mind for our class are chances to spread positivity to others and within yourselves. You all know the feeling: a small act of kindness can mean the world for one person, but it can also improve your own mentality. Just seeing that you’re able to improve the lives of others no matter how little is enough to lift you up. Spread the kindness-- it's contagious, and soon enough you'll realize that the smallest acts can create the largest ripple.

A Little More About Me!

In the end, the best way you can judge how I will perform as your class secretary is to get a know me a little better. :")

But really, there's not much to say: I'm just another student trying to make my way through high school and enjoy the process.

I really, REALLY enjoy Wushu, and I've performed during Multicultural week as well as served as the air band leader for this year's HC Fight Set!

When I can't go out and practice martial arts, I spend my time on YouTube searching up inspiration for art and graphic design. I've liked to draw ever since I was little, and I'm glad that I can still use my interest to help our class. I helped our class officers out this year, designing both the class logo and windbreaker.

But one thing I'd really like to address further is my personal belief on how we can best tackle high school together.

As we continue our high school career, we’re overwhelmed with more and more stress each year. The sad truth is that it just doesn’t get easier. But when the roads get rougher and the nights feel longer, we really need our peers to be there and help us pull through. It’s when we reach our darkest and lowest times that we begin to realize just how much our relationships matter. Students often get too caught up with school and fail to see that within all the all-nighters pulled and group projects clutched, there’s still something that makes high school all the worthwhile: the bonds and memories we share with our class. I believe that with the help of other officers, we can supply our class with countless opportunities to create those much needed memories,

good vibes, and relationships that we’ll treasure forever. What really gets you far in life are built upon the connections you share with people you can depend on. It’ll get rough, but I truly want our class to take what we have and make the most out of it. No, these changes aren’t going to happen overnight. But through constant commitment, we will succeed in improving our high school experience, step by step. What I’m asking from you, as a fellow student, is to give me the opportunity to make these changes.

These changes alone aren’t going to make junior year any easier instantly--it’s a process that requires the help of the students, and in turn we’ll be able to discover the positive aspects of high school itself. I hope that even if I can’t change our student mentality on school, I can at least help expand your perspectives and shine light onto your junior year experience.

Thanks for making it this far and spending time to read my ideas for next year!
